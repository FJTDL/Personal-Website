After the success of the de-glitching network, I have since moved into the Neural Posterior Estimation side of the problem. Here, I am to esimate the initial angular velocity, and the ratio of the rotational kinetic energy to the gravitational potential amplitude. Wordy, for sure, so just know:

$$\beta = \frac{T}{|W|}$$

Where $T =$ rotational kinetic energy, and $\lvert W \rvert =$ gravitational potential magnitude.

The problem I'm having? My signals are *extremely* thin. It means when I use any form of pooling I wash out the signal completely, and that while strided convolutions have historically been my closest ally, they result in the model rapidly overfitting as it recognises my limited 1705 signals.

It's a problem, but we'll fix it eventually!
